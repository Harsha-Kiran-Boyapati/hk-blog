Shakespeare's Venus and Adonis is a narrative poem published in 1593, telling the story of Venus's unrequited love for the beautiful Adonis. The poem explores themes of desire, beauty, rejection, and the inversion of traditional gender roles in courtship.

This analysis provides detailed examination of each stanza, breaking down Shakespeare's complex language and imagery to make the poem more accessible to modern readers.
